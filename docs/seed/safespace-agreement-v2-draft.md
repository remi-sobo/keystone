## Consulting Services Agreement

**SOBO Consulting LLC and SafeSpace**
**Effective date: July 15, 2026**

This Consulting Services Agreement (the "Agreement") is between SOBO Consulting LLC ("SOBO") and SafeSpace. It lays out the work SOBO will do over the next six months, what SafeSpace will own at the end, how we will pay for it, and how the two of us will work together.

The first ten years built SafeSpace into what it is today. This engagement is about the next ten: putting the systems, skills, and fundraising rhythms in place so the organization runs strong and stays rooted in this community for years to come.

### 1. Term

This engagement runs for six months, from July 15, 2026 through January 15, 2027. The two of us can agree in writing to extend or adjust it.

### 2. Scope of Services

SOBO Consulting will deliver the following, using its five-part method: Diagnose, Design, Build, Train, and Stabilize.

**a. Operating hub**

SOBO will configure and deliver a SafeSpace instance of BloomOS, SOBO's operating system for small nonprofits. Throughout this Agreement, "the hub" means that instance. The hub will include:

- Strategy and goals, projects and tasks.
- A donor CRM with a pipeline and stages, and a grants pipeline.
- Dedicated areas for program, finance, data, and compliance.
- A board login with access SafeSpace controls.
- Role-based permissions, so each person sees only what their role requires.

The platform is built on standard, widely used infrastructure: Next.js and TypeScript, a PostgreSQL database hosted on Supabase, deployed on Vercel. Ownership and licensing are covered in Section 7, data protection in Section 8, and hosting in Section 9.

**b. Fundraising strategy and systems**

SOBO will build, and SafeSpace will walk away with:

- A written fundraising strategy, including a gift table and the mix of strategies for the year.
- An annual fundraising calendar.
- A donor pipeline and a grants pipeline, live in the hub.
- Documented donor journeys covering cultivation, solicitation, and stewardship.
- A case for support.
- Donor communication templates.
- Fundraising collateral: a pitch deck, a send deck, and a one-pager.

The strategy includes a major-donor component: prospect identification, qualification, cultivation, solicitation, and stewardship, along with the week-to-week rhythms to run it.

**c. Developing the leaders**

Working sessions with Aris and Jasmine, with prework and homework tied to real fundraising tasks, pitch practice, and live fundraising reps, so they can lead fundraising and operations independently.

- Estimated time commitment: 4 to 6 hours per week each, covering sessions, homework, and implementation. Expect 6 to 8 hours per week in the first month, when sessions run twice weekly and the workload is front-loaded. These are estimates offered for planning, not fixed requirements.

**d. Board fundraising support**

So the board can support fundraising in the way each director is comfortable with, SOBO will provide:

- Up to two board-facing working sessions, covering connection mapping, prospect identification, and how board members can open doors.
- A board fundraising toolkit: talking points, introduction email templates, and a shareable one-pager.
- Drafting support for individual directors' outreach to their own networks.

This work is about connections and introductions. It does not assign fundraising targets or quotas to any director, and participation is at each director's discretion. Ongoing board governance consulting and board recruitment are not included.

**e. Impact and evaluation**

- An impact and evaluation framework, led by Dr. Kendra Sobomehin, that turns the work SafeSpace already does into the evidence funders ask for.

**f. Financial and administrative support**

Led by Shannon Fair. This is the financial infrastructure that fundraising depends on. It is not bookkeeping and does not replace SafeSpace's bookkeeper. Shannon will coordinate with the bookkeeper rather than duplicate that work. Included:

- A three-year operating budget and an annual budget, built with the team, so multi-year asks are grounded in real numbers.
- A review of the chart of accounts so program, administrative, and fundraising costs are separated the way funders and the Form 990 expect.
- The finance area of the hub, showing budget against actuals and runway.
- Grant budget templates and the financial reporting formats funders ask for.
- A compliance calendar covering filings, registrations, renewals, and board governance dates.
- Core policies that fundraising depends on, including gift acceptance, expense, and document retention.

If SafeSpace already has any of the above in good shape, the two of us will agree in writing to drop it and redirect that time to fundraising. The fee does not change.

### 3. What Success Looks Like

So both sides measure this the same way. Success has five parts. SOBO is responsible for parts a through c. Part d is shared, and is measured openly rather than assumed.

**a. The deliverables exist.** Everything listed in Section 2 has been built and handed over.

**b. Aris and Jasmine can teach it back.** Not a quiz, a teach-back. Each of them can deliver the five-minute pitch unaided, present the fundraising strategy and explain the reasoning behind it to Susan and the board, and say why each part of the system exists.

**c. The system is documented and live.** The fundraising strategy and the operations plan both exist in writing and live in the hub. The operations plan names the weekly, monthly, and quarterly rhythms and who owns each one. The donor and grant pipelines are current. The practical test: if a new person joined SafeSpace, they could pick up the system and run it.

**d. Execution is measured and reported.** Through the engagement, SOBO tracks and reports to SafeSpace whether the rhythms are actually running: are the weekly fundraising blocks happening, are donor journeys being worked, are the pipelines current, and have Aris and Jasmine each run the full cycle of preparing for, leading, following up on, and rating a donor meeting. SOBO reports activities completed, not dollars raised, for the reasons in Section 12. Execution depends on the SafeSpace team. If execution is still building at the end of the term, SOBO stays available as a resource under Section 9.

**e. Confidence is tracked, not graded.** Aris and Jasmine will each rate their own confidence across the core fundraising competencies at the start of the engagement, monthly, and at the end. This is reported as part of the impact and evaluation framework so growth is visible across the six months. It is a growth measure, not a pass or fail gate.

### 4. Out of Scope

Anything not listed in Section 2 is outside this Agreement. In particular:

- A redesign of the SafeSpace website. It is available as a separate engagement if SafeSpace decides to pursue it later.
- Bookkeeping, payroll, and transaction processing, which remain with SafeSpace's bookkeeper.
- Ongoing board governance consulting and board recruitment.
- Hosting and support after the included year, which are addressed in Section 9.

### 5. What SafeSpace Provides

- Timely access to the data, accounts, and donor information needed to do the work.
- A single point of contact and decisions made in a reasonable timeframe so the work keeps moving.
- The consents, permissions, and legal basis for any personal information SafeSpace places in the hub, as set out in Section 7.

### 7. Ownership of Work Product, Platform License, and Data

**a. What SafeSpace owns.** Any work product created specifically for SafeSpace under this Agreement becomes the property of SafeSpace on completion of the engagement and payment in full. That includes its customized workflows and configuration, templates, dashboards, fundraising strategy and materials, AI prompts written for SafeSpace, documentation, and reports. SafeSpace may use, change, and keep using all of it with no ongoing obligation to SOBO.

**b. What SOBO owns.** SOBO retains ownership of its pre-existing and independently developed intellectual property, including the BloomOS platform and its codebase, and SOBO's methodologies, frameworks, proprietary tools, and templates. These were not developed for SafeSpace and are used across SOBO's clients.

**c. SafeSpace's license to the platform.** SafeSpace receives a perpetual, non-exclusive, royalty-free license to use its BloomOS instance, and any SOBO materials incorporated into the delivered system, for its own internal operations. The license survives the end of this Agreement.

To be clear about the distinction: the license is the right to use the software. Access to the live, hosted hub is a service that SOBO provides. That service is included for the first year under Section 9. After that, continued access to the live hosted hub requires an active BloomOS plan. If SafeSpace chooses not to continue a plan, the license remains, and SafeSpace receives a full export of its data and transition support under Section 9(e). SafeSpace is never locked in by its own data.

**d. SafeSpace's data.** All data SafeSpace puts into the hub, or that is generated by its use, belongs to SafeSpace. SafeSpace keeps full access to its data and its hub, including the ability to export all organizational data in a commonly used format such as CSV or JSON, at any time, whether or not any ongoing support arrangement continues. On request, SOBO will return or delete SafeSpace data.

### 8. Data Protection, Privacy, and Security

SafeSpace is the owner and controller of the data in the hub. SOBO acts as a service provider and processes that data only to deliver the services described here.

**a. Before any youth information enters the hub.** No identifiable youth information will be placed in the hub until both of the following are complete:

- The two of us agree in writing on exactly what participant information will be stored and why, field by field.
- SafeSpace confirms that its existing parent or guardian consents cover that use, and obtains any additional consent needed.

Until that step is finished, the hub will hold organizational data only.

**b. How youth information is handled.**

- **Minimum necessary.** The hub holds only the participant information SafeSpace needs to run and evaluate its program. Clinical records, diagnoses, therapy notes, and crisis-incident details will not be stored in the hub unless both sides agree in writing and additional safeguards are put in place first.
- **Restricted access.** Individual youth records are visible only to designated SafeSpace program staff. Board members, and users whose role is limited to fundraising, finance, or development, cannot see individual youth records.
- **No AI processing of youth information.** Individually identifiable youth information will not be sent to or processed by AI models. AI features operate on organizational data, or on de-identified or aggregated information.

**c. Security measures.**

- Encryption of data in transit and at rest.
- Row-level access controls and least-privilege, role-based permissions.
- Unique named accounts, with no shared logins.
- Access limited to SOBO personnel who need it to perform the services.

**d. Infrastructure providers.** The hub runs on third-party infrastructure, currently Supabase for the database and Vercel for hosting. SOBO will keep SafeSpace informed of the providers it relies on and will not add a provider that materially changes how SafeSpace data is handled without telling SafeSpace first.

**e. Security incidents.** If SOBO becomes aware of a breach of security affecting SafeSpace data, SOBO will notify SafeSpace without undue delay and no later than 72 hours after becoming aware of it, and will cooperate with SafeSpace on any notifications required by law.

**f. No regulatory certification.** SOBO does not represent that the hub is certified under HIPAA, FERPA, or any other regulatory framework. SafeSpace is responsible for determining which legal obligations apply to it. If SafeSpace needs the hub to meet a specific framework, the two of us will scope that separately.

### 9. Hosting, Support, and the Platform After the Engagement

**a. The first year is included.** Hosting, maintenance, security updates, and support for the hub are included in the fee under Section 6 for twelve months, beginning the day the hub goes live. There is nothing further to pay during that period.

**b. After the first year.** SafeSpace may continue on a published BloomOS plan, month to month, with no long-term commitment. SafeSpace's plan is Bloom Grow, at the published rate, currently $250 per month. SafeSpace's rate is held at $250 per month for five years from the end of the included year, even if published pricing changes. SafeSpace may cancel at any time.

**c. What the plan covers.** Hosting and infrastructure, backups, security updates, platform upgrades and new features as they ship, and support. Other BloomOS plans are available if SafeSpace wants more or less.

**d. If SafeSpace chooses not to continue.** SafeSpace keeps its license and its data under Section 7. SOBO is not obligated to keep hosting the live hub without an active plan. SafeSpace would receive the export and transition support in Section 9(e).

**e. What transition support means.** If SafeSpace leaves, or if SOBO stops supporting the platform, SOBO will provide, at no cost:

- A full export of all SafeSpace data in CSV or JSON, plus any stored documents and files.
- A written data dictionary explaining the structure of the export, so another vendor or staff member can read and map it.
- Up to 10 hours of transition support, by call or in writing, to help SafeSpace or its new vendor understand the data and plan the move.
- Delivery of the export within 30 days of SafeSpace's written request.

Transition support beyond 10 hours is available at SOBO's standard hourly rate. If SOBO ever stops supporting the platform, SOBO will give SafeSpace at least 90 days' written notice before doing so.

**f. Continued consulting.** SOBO stays available as a resource after the six months. Whether that continues as informal support, a board role, or a further engagement is up to the two of us.

### 10. Confidentiality

Each side will protect the other's non-public information and use it only to do the work described here. Donor data, participant data, and organizational records stay confidential and are handled with care.

### 11. Independent Contractor

SOBO is an independent contractor, not an employee of SafeSpace. SOBO is responsible for its own taxes and carries no employee benefits under this Agreement.

### 12. Results and No Guarantee

SOBO brings proven methods, real effort, and experienced hands. Fundraising outcomes also depend on things outside SOBO's control, including, but not limited to, how consistently the SafeSpace team executes. For that reason, SOBO does not guarantee a specific dollar amount raised. What SOBO commits to is the philosophy, the system, and the coaching that put SafeSpace in the best position to raise well, as described in Section 3.

### 13. Limitation of Liability

Neither side is liable to the other for indirect or consequential damages. SOBO's total liability under this Agreement is limited to the fees SafeSpace has paid under it.

### 14. Conflict of Interest and Board Approval

Remi Sobomehin, SOBO's principal, serves on the SafeSpace board. He disclosed his interest in this engagement and recused himself from the decision. The disinterested members of the board reviewed the terms and approved the engagement on July 13, 2026, finding it fair and in SafeSpace's best interest. This Agreement reflects an arm's-length arrangement at SOBO's standard rate.

### 15. Termination

Either side may end this Agreement with 30 days' written notice. If that happens, SafeSpace pays for the work delivered through the end of the notice period, prorated, and SOBO hands over everything completed to that point.

### 16. General

This Agreement is the entire understanding between the two sides and replaces any earlier discussions. Changes must be in writing and agreed by both. It is governed by the laws of the State of California. It may be signed electronically and in counterparts, and an electronic signature has the same effect as a handwritten one.
