## Consulting Services Agreement

**SOBO Consulting LLC and SafeSpace Center, Inc.**
**Effective date: July 15, 2026**

This Consulting Services Agreement (the "Agreement") is between SOBO Consulting LLC ("Sobo Consulting") and SafeSpace Center, Inc. ("SafeSpace"). It lays out the work Sobo Consulting will do over the next six months, what SafeSpace will own at the end, how we will pay for it, and how the two of us will work together.

The first ten years built SafeSpace into what it is today. This engagement is about the next ten: putting the systems, skills, and fundraising rhythms in place so the organization runs strong and stays rooted in this community for years to come.

### 1. Term

This engagement runs for six months, from July 10, 2026 through January 10, 2027. The two of us can agree in writing to extend or adjust it.

### 2. Scope of Services

Sobo Consulting will deliver the following four workstreams, using its five-part method: Diagnose, Design, Build, Train, and Stabilize.

**a. Build the system (led by Remi Sobomehin)**

- **The operating hub.** A custom platform that runs the organization from one place: strategy and goals, projects and tasks, a full CRM with a donor pipeline and stages, a grants pipeline, and dedicated areas for program, finance, data, and compliance. A board login with access SafeSpace controls, so the board sees what SafeSpace wants it to see.
- **The platform, plainly.** The hub is a custom build on the system Sobo Consulting runs for its own youth nonprofit, hosted on managed cloud infrastructure with role-based access controls and encryption in transit and at rest. It is not a per-seat software license. SafeSpace owns the system and the data inside it as described in Section 7.
- **Fundraising strategy.** A written fundraising strategy SafeSpace owns, including a major donor component covering prospect identification, cultivation, and solicitation; an annual fundraising calendar; a gift table and segmentation strategy; a case for support; and donor templates for outreach, thank-you, and follow-up.
- **Fundraising systems and collateral.** Donor journeys, the week-to-week stewardship and cultivation rhythms, and the fundraising collateral: a pitch deck, a send deck, and a one-pager.

**b. Develop the leaders (led by Remi Sobomehin)**

- Working sessions with Aris and Jasmine, with prework and homework tied to real fundraising tasks, pitch practice, and live fundraising reps, so they can lead fundraising and operations independently.
- Plan on four to six hours a week from each of them: the session, the homework, and implementation time. Expect six to eight in the first month, when sessions run twice a week and the work is front-loaded. It is training, and these are estimates for planning, not requirements.
- **Board fundraising engagement.** A board fundraising toolkit, an introductions playbook, and up to two working sessions with the board, so board members can identify connections, make introductions, and support donor cultivation. Ongoing board governance consulting and board recruitment are not included.

**c. Show the impact (led by Dr. Kendra Sobomehin)**

- An impact and evaluation framework that turns the work SafeSpace already does into the evidence funders ask for, with funder-ready artifacts, wired into the hub.

**d. Hold the back office (led by Shannon Fair)**

- Setup support for financial management and compliance, so the back end is solid as the organization grows.

### 3. Deliverables and Success Measures

The deliverables above are the walk-away list. In month one, the two of us will set written success measures for each workstream, each with a baseline and the evidence that shows it is done. The measures live in the shared engagement room both sides can see, and they are updated with evidence as the work ships, so we are all measuring success the same way for the full six months.

### 4. Out of Scope

Anything not listed above is outside this Agreement. In particular:

- A redesign of the SafeSpace website is not included. It is available as a separate engagement.
- The Youth Action Board portal (applications, roster, onboarding, hours, communications) is not included. It is available as a separate engagement.
- Support beyond the six-month term is not included. It is available as a separate engagement, scoped on its own.
- Program design stays with the SafeSpace program team. Fundraising touches every function, so the work will reach all of it, but this engagement does not run programs.

### 5. What SafeSpace Provides

- Participation from Aris, Jasmine, and Susan in the working sessions, and completion of prework and homework.
- Timely access to the data, accounts, and donor information needed to do the work.
- A single point of contact and decisions made in a reasonable timeframe so the work keeps moving.

### 7. Ownership of Work Product

SafeSpace owns everything created specifically for it under this Agreement, in perpetuity, once the engagement completes and the full fee is paid. That includes the operating hub and its configuration, all data inside it, customized systems and workflows, templates, dashboards, fundraising materials, AI prompts and assistants configured for SafeSpace inside the hub, documentation, and reports. SafeSpace can keep using, changing, and maintaining all of it with no ongoing obligation to Sobo Consulting.

Sobo Consulting keeps ownership of its pre-existing methodologies, frameworks, templates, tools, and the platforms and software it builds and operates, developed independently of this engagement and reused across clients. None of that is exclusive to SafeSpace. SafeSpace receives a perpetual, non-exclusive license to use any such materials that are part of the delivered system, for its internal operations.

SafeSpace keeps full access to its data and the customized hub, including the ability to export all organizational data in commonly used formats, at any time and after the engagement ends, regardless of whether ongoing support services continue.

### 8. Confidentiality and Data Security

Each side will protect the other's non-public information and use it only to do the work described here. Donor data and organizational records stay confidential and are handled with care.

Donor, financial, HR, and youth information lives inside the hub, in areas SafeSpace controls, visible only to the people SafeSpace authorizes, behind role-based access controls with encryption in transit and at rest. The consulting engagement record holds decisions, commitments, and work product; it does not hold donor intelligence, youth records, or personnel files.

The signed agreement adds specifics for youth and other sensitive information:

- **Minimum necessary.** The hub holds only the participant information SafeSpace needs to run and evaluate its program. Clinical records, diagnoses, therapy notes, and crisis-incident details are not stored unless both sides agree in writing and additional safeguards are in place first.
- **Consent and legal basis.** SafeSpace is responsible for the consents required to collect and use participant information, including parent or guardian consent where required.
- **Restricted access.** Individual youth records are visible only to designated SafeSpace program staff. Board members, and users whose role is limited to fundraising, finance, or development, cannot see individual youth records.
- **No AI processing of youth information.** Individually identifiable youth information is never sent to or processed by AI models. AI features operate on organizational data, or on de-identified or aggregated information.
- **Security incidents.** If Sobo Consulting becomes aware of a breach affecting SafeSpace data, it will notify SafeSpace without undue delay and no later than 72 hours after becoming aware of it.
- **No regulatory certification.** Sobo Consulting does not represent that the hub is certified under HIPAA, FERPA, or any other framework. SafeSpace determines which legal obligations apply to it, and any specific framework is scoped separately.

### 9. Independent Contractor

Sobo Consulting is an independent contractor, not an employee of SafeSpace. Sobo Consulting is responsible for its own taxes and carries no employee benefits under this Agreement.

### 10. Results and No Guarantee

Sobo Consulting brings proven methods, real effort, and experienced hands. Fundraising outcomes also depend on things outside Sobo Consulting's control, including how consistently the SafeSpace team executes. For that reason, Sobo Consulting does not guarantee a specific dollar amount raised. What Sobo Consulting commits to is the philosophy, the system, and the coaching that put SafeSpace in the best position to raise well, measured against the written success measures in Section 3.

### 11. Limitation of Liability

Neither side is liable to the other for indirect or consequential damages. Sobo Consulting's total liability under this Agreement is limited to the fees SafeSpace has paid under it.

### 12. Conflict of Interest and Board Approval

Remi Sobomehin, Sobo Consulting's principal, serves on the SafeSpace board. He disclosed his interest in this engagement and recused himself from the decision. The disinterested members of the board reviewed the terms and approved the engagement on [approval date], finding it fair and in SafeSpace's best interest. This Agreement reflects an arm's-length arrangement at Sobo Consulting's standard rate.

### 13. Hosting, Support, and the Platform After the Engagement

**a. The first year is included.** Hosting, customized design, maintenance, security updates, and support for SafeSpace's BloomOS instance are included in the fee under Section 6 for twelve months, beginning the day the instance goes live. There is nothing further to pay during that period.

**b. After the first year.** SafeSpace may continue on a published BloomOS plan, month to month, with no long-term commitment. SafeSpace's plan is Bloom Grow, at the published rate, currently $250 per month. SafeSpace's rate is held at $250 per month for five years from the end of the included year, even if published pricing changes. SafeSpace may cancel at any time.

**c. What the plan covers.** Hosting and infrastructure, backups, security updates, platform upgrades and new features as they ship, and support. Other BloomOS plans are available if SafeSpace wants more or less.

**d. If SafeSpace chooses not to continue.** SafeSpace keeps its license and its data under Section 7. Sobo Consulting is not obligated to keep hosting the instance without a plan in place, so SafeSpace would receive a full export of all its data in a common format, along with reasonable help moving to another system. SafeSpace will never be locked in by its own data.

**e. If Sobo Consulting stops supporting the platform.** Sobo Consulting will give SafeSpace at least 90 days' notice, provide a full export, and offer reasonable help moving to another system.

**f. Continued consulting.** Sobo Consulting stays available as a resource after the six months. Whether that continues as informal support, a board role, or a further engagement is up to the two of us.

### 14. Termination

Either side may end this Agreement with 30 days' written notice. If that happens, SafeSpace pays for the work delivered through the end of the notice period, prorated, and Sobo Consulting hands over everything completed to that point, including the data access and export rights in Section 7 for everything delivered.

### 15. General

This Agreement is the entire understanding between the two sides and replaces any earlier discussions. Changes must be in writing and agreed by both. It is governed by the laws of the State of California. It may be signed electronically and in counterparts, and an electronic signature has the same effect as a handwritten one.
